Passwords, Please

Passwords, Please is part of a campaign to create an educational puzzle game geared towards engaging players with ideas of how people store personal data and how others might take advantage of poor information security habits.

Project began in March 2019 and is currently in ongoing development with a planned release by the university May 1, 2020.

  • Developed prototypes and framework for player/camera movement and click interactions

One of the features I am most proud of working on is the point-and-click design paired with a lock-and-key puzzle structure, which keeps the player primarily focused on the puzzle solving and creates a more user-friendly experience for our audience. Passwords, Please was originally a first-person platformer that focused on having the player manipulate the environment to access new areas. After analysis of playtesting data, I found that our users were beginning to lose sight of the game's intended purpose and gameplay design became increasingly difficult due to confusion on what the goal was. Players would focus not on the themes or topics expressed by the puzzle, but rather unnecessary aspects such as how to move with the keyboard/mouse, or have a strong desire to ignore the puzzle completely and try clicking on every irrelevant object.

To address this problem, I created a new prototype focused only on clicking (akin to 2D flash games), where every action the player had could be accomplished with only one button. This served to reduce the confusion involving controls, reduce the technical complexity our old design originally had, as well as bring the design focus back on the message the puzzles are trying to convey- that being how users can recognize and understand poor security habits.
