OddestSea

A 3D sailing game where monsters are in constant pursuit and the player's only hope of survival is to reach the dormant lighthouses scattered throughout a dark and deadly sea.

I work as a designer who provides technical support when needed, such as developing rapid prototypes to test new mechanics. Some of my primary work includes crafting and balancing elements and mechanics in accordance to the game vision and core themes, as well as detecting design flaws and making adjustments so all aspects of the experience fall in accordance to our core design pillars. I am focused mainly on systems design but I collaborate closely with all members of the development pipeline to discuss and review content, sprint plans, and cultivate a healthy collaborative environment.

Project began in August 2018 and is currently in ongoing development with a team of 13. Expected release on Steam Spring 2020.

My contribution to OddestSea:

  • Designing many aspects of the game including gameplay, systems, environments, upgrades, etc.
  • I worked closely with programmers to iterate on gameplay features; movement, cameras, flare, player stats, & other mechanics.
  • Rapidly developing and implementing essential UI and mechanics for iterative playtesting and adjustments.
  • Addressing design flaws and collaborating with team members to prevent conflicts and confusion down the pipeline.
  • Organizing clear design documentation and core vision to all members of the team.

The Ripper

A 2D dialogue-based puzzle game based on the infamous 19th century serial killer Jack the Ripper and historical figure Inspector Frederick Abberline. The police are hot on the trail of the most famous criminal of the century, but the player must locate Jack's accomplice before they can take him down once and for all.

This game was a small solo project to both familiarize myself with various development pipelines, and create a fun little experience based off of one of my favorite historical periods.

Some design difficulties I ran into included how to display held clues to the player and how to provide a sense of progression. I solved these problems by designing a diegetic UI and layered music track to add more instruments with each clue found. Project was finished in approximately 2 months.

My contribution to The Ripper:

  • Designed the puzzle and array-based dialogue system
  • Designed a diegetic UI to enhance the player experience of playing as a clever detective
  • Created all art assets, sound & music, and dialogue

"Passwords, Please"

A 3D escape room experience where the player takes on the role of a spy who breaks into a futuristic IT company and must steal private information to reach the company vault.

I am currently working as a contracted designer for the Indiana University IT department to create a game geared towards engaging players with the idea of how people store personal data, and how others might take advantage of poor information security habits. This would include things such as passwords, passphrases, authentication methods, and other personal information that may be used to gain access to otherwise private or classified material.

I work closely with contract supervisors and my contract partner (programmer) to make sure all aspects of the design fall within institution credentials. My design work thus far has primarily been focused on puzzle and level design.

Above all, I want to create a fun experience focused on keeping players engaged with the puzzle solving involved rather than creating a purely educational simulator, as I believe this is crucial for keeping the experience both memorable, but also enjoyable. However, I do not want to undermine the educational aspects of gameplay, as these too are crucial for delivering the message we want players to walk away with-- that is, to make players feel aware of their own security practices and how they might change them.

My contribution to "Passwords, Please":

  • Designing a linear escape room experience with non-linear branches to create a layer of logical depth
  • Designing and developing prototypes for various minor mechanics, tools, and demos such as tutorial levels, hint indicators, etc.
  • Organizing and updating clear and concise game documentation
  • Communicating regularly with contractors and team members to plan out tasks and confirm design directions
