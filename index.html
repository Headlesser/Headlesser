Emilie Holtz - Game Designer

My name is Emilie Holtz and I am a Game Designer with a thorough understanding of scripting. This allows me to communicate and work efficiently with both designers and programmers at a high level. I tackle difficult design challenges with a criticle eye and a strong attention to detail. I constantly seek out the wisdom of incredibly smart and creative people to strengthen my skills and expand my knowledge on all aspects of design and game development.

OddestSea | August 2018 - Ongoing

A 3D sailing game where monsters are in constant pursuit and the player's only hope of survival is to reach the lighthouse at the center of a dark and deadly sea.

OddestSea was created with a team of 11 people, using Unity Engine and C#. I work as a designer who provides technical support in the form of scripting (player movement, camera movement) and facilitating communications between the tech team and other members of the group. As a designer I have collaborated with others to create enemies, player interactions, level layouts. Click here for more information.

Passwords, Please | March 2019 - Ongoing

A 3D puzzle game created for the IT Training Program at Indiana University. The goal of this game is to make players feel aware of their own security practices and how they might change them.

Passwords, Please is being developed by a team of 3 people using Unity Engine and C#. I work as a designer and consultant with supervisors to make sure all aspects of the design fall within institution credentials and goals. As a designer I have worked on developing the puzzles and point-and-click structure. Documentation on this project can be found by clicking here. Click here for more information.
